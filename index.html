Basic Strategy

To play blackjack well, first you need to learn Basic Strategy, also known as "The Chart", below. Then you need to practice Basic Strategy. Next you can choose to learn card counting if you have the time and are very serious, but not before you learn Basic Strategy extremely well and can use it confidently, quickly, and although I don't encourage it, intoxicated. General Rules

  • Always stand on a hard 17 or greater
  • Always stand on a soft 19 (A,8) or greater
  • Always split A,A and 8,8
  • Never split 10,10 and 5,5

Chart Observations

  • When the dealer is showing a 7 or better, and you have 12-16, you have to take the risk and hit
  • If the dealer's face card is 2-6, generally you stand (if you risk busting) or double (if you do not risk busting)
  • A hard 10 and 5,5 are treated the same way, as you might expect--double except when the dealer shows a 10 or an ace
  • 10s and 11s are almost always doubled
  • 9s are almost always split
  • 2s, 3s, and 7s are split for dealer face card 2-7
  • Generally double soft hands (Ace paired with 2-7) and a hard 9 when the dealer is showing a 4-6
  • for hard totals 12-16 when the dealer is showing 2-6, the only time you would ever hit is if you have a 12, and only then when the dealer shows a 2 or 3
  • Split and double when you should! This reduces the casino's edge

Learning Basic Strategy

These steps will help you learn Basic Strategy.

  1. Study the chart, while noting the general rules and chart observations above. Think about how the patterns make sense, and if a play does not make sense, you need to try to figure out why it would and convince yourself it does.
  2. Then get a deck of cards and practice, referring back to the chart when you are not sure. Flash cards are also suggested at this step. (Ex: front of card says "10" back of card says "Double vs. 2-9 otherwise Hit")
  3. When you are feeling pretty confident, play with a friend, preferably one who knows the chart, or use the Strategy Trainer (Click "Free Blackjack Game: Blackjack Strategy Trainer")
